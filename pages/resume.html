Harvard Medical School & Massachusetts General Hospital
Boston, MA
Research Fellow
Apr 2015 – Present

  • Developer of TOPAS (Monte Carlo simulation of radiation therapy); improved QA by introducing issue tracking, CI, a custom numerical regression test suite, and code coverage metrics; migrated documentation from a Pages document to a website; implemented features such as multithreading, enhanced support of patient data files, and output of 2D data structures
  • Devised an algorithm to sample directions within an elliptical cone and verified its uniformity using a two-level Kolmogorov-Smirnov test; this was used to model the incoming proton therapy beams in patient simulations
  • Created models to identify patients whose anatomy permits enhanced benefits from proton therapy, by exploiting knowledge learned from historic treatment plans; this enables better-informed referral decisions at cancer centers without proton therapy capabilities
  • Implemented a custom regression model in scikit-learn and compared the cross-validated accuracy and training/prediction times to a random forest model; models were validated for skull-base (41 patients), lung (104 pts), and prostate (119 pts) cancer cohorts
  • Audited MIT course: 6.036 Introduction to Machine Learning

University of Oxford & CERN

Postdoctoral Research Assistant
Jun 2014 – Mar 2015

  • Team leader of 5 CERN physicists to infer the underlying kinematics of the Higgs boson by deconvolving detector effects using an iterative Bayesian approach
  • Organized weekly meetings to review the latest results from each team member, identify potential problems, and delegate duties for future work
  • Software Coordinator for the ATLAS Monte Carlo Group, which involved developing new features and interfacing C++/Python code with external C++ and Fortran libraries
  • Liased with Software Coordinators of other key groups and the Release Manager to deliver an operational simulation framework before Run 2 of the Large Hadron Collider

University of Oxford & CERN
Oxford, UK & Geneva, Switzerland
Doctoral Student
Oct 2010 – Jun 2014

  • Core team member of the flagship search for the Higgs boson at the Large Hadron Collider
  • Processed petabytes of empirical and simulated data with worldwide distributed computing
  • Built a statistical model describing the contributions from signal and background processes, as constrained and validated by observations in "control regions"
  • Devised and validated new techniques that contributed to a 50% improvement in the expected Z-score (the most sensitive standalone measurement of the Higgs boson to date); these techniques were quickly adopted by other analysis groups and our competitor experiment
  • Investigated radiation induced attenuation of optical fibres at low temperatures, which required online monitoring and offline time series analysis with fibre-dependent time lags
  • Regularly presented findings at collaboration meetings and international conferences
  • Spoke at science outreach events to audiences ranging from 6-year-olds to postgraduates and volunteered at science exhibitions at the Royal Society and London Science Museum
  • Undergraduate teaching: 3rd year sub-atomic physics tutorials and 2nd year optics laboratory
